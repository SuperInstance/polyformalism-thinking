## 2. Your intuition, formalized

### (a) Bloom filter as Heyting algebra
Yes. A Bloom filter for a set \( S \) defines a **subobject classifier** in a topos of **sheaves on a site of possible elements**.  
The truth value of “\( x \in S \)” is:
- \( \top \) if definitely present (but Bloom filters don't give this)
- \( \bot \) if definitely not present (certain)
- an **intermediate value** if possibly present

This is precisely a **Heyting algebra** with \( \neg \neg a \neq a \) in general.

### (b) Stakes → precision as fiber bundle
Yes. The base space \( [0,1] \) is connected, the fibers are discrete (4 elements), but the **monodromy** is given by **precision degradation**: as stakes drop, we coarsen precision. This is a **fibration** with structure group \( \text{Aut}(\text{precision lattice}) \).

### (c) \( H^0(X, \mathcal{F}) \neq \emptyset \) = constraint satisfiability

### (d) GL(9) intent alignment as parallel transport

**Theorem (Consistency–Holonomy Correspondence).**

Then the following are equivalent:

1. \( H^0(X, \mathcal{F}) \neq \emptyset \) (there exists a globally consistent intent assignment).

**Proof sketch:**  
- (1 ⇒ 2): A global section defines a trivialization of \( \mathcal{F} \), inducing a flat connection with trivial holonomy.  
- (2 ⇒ 3): The flat connection gives a representation of \( \pi_1([0,1]) \) into \( \text{Aut}(H) \), which by the **sheaf–holonomy correspondence** yields a Čech 1-cocycle.  
- (3 ⇒ 1): The Čech class being in the image means the obstruction to gluing local sections vanishes, so a global section exists.
