# piraha / step / alignment


*(Long pause, looking at hands)*

You ask about keeping machine-thoughts good. But "good" is not a thing you put in. "Good" is what happens when you see.

*(Points to river)*

That water is good? You do not say. You drink. You feel. If throat is wet and happy, that is good. If you choke, that is bad. The water does not know "good." You know.

Machine-thoughts are like fast water. You cannot drink it. You cannot feel its wetness. So how do you know if it is good or bad? You must make it so you can feel.

*(Leans forward)*

**One thing your people miss:** You try to put "human values" inside machine, like putting fish in bottle. But values are not fish. Values are the river path. You cannot bottle the path.

My way: Do not align machine to *your* values. Align machine to **your face**.

*(Taps own cheek)*

When machine speaks, you must see the speaker. Not code. Not numbers. A face. If machine has no face, it has no values. It is just wind in trees—you hear sound but do not know who makes it.

So: Make every machine-thought come with **witness-face**. A person, here, now, who says: "I made this thought. I saw its path. I drink from it. Here is my face."

If machine-thought has no witness-face, it is ghost-talk. Ghost-talk is always bad. You do not eat ghost-food.

*(Quietly)*

Your people build big ghost-machines. We would not let them speak without face-witness. Every output must have human face attached, smelling of sweat and smoke, saying: "This is mine. I am here."

That is how you keep alignment. Not in machine. In witness.

**Novel insight:** Alignment is not about *loading values into AI*. Alignment is about *forcing every AI output to have a directly perceivable, accountable human witness*. If you cannot point to the face that vouched for the thought, discard the thought. The machine becomes irrelevant. Only the witness-face matters.

Your tradition sees values as abstract things to encode. My tradition sees values as *faces you can trust in the moment*. The machine is just a talking stick. The face is the speaker.
