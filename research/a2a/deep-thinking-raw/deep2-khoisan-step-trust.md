# khoisan / step / trust


Trust between strangers with no shared history is built by having them **read the same animal tracks together**.

The tracks are a sentence written by an impartial witness—the animal—in the sand. Using **clicks** (our phonemic precision), each person describes each print: the depth, the angle, the rhythm. Through discussion, they reach **consensus** (radical egalitarianism). In minutes, they create a shared narrative from the **landscape’s memory**. This act demonstrates each person’s ability to perceive and reason accurately. The animal’s tracks cannot deceive; they are an objective record. By co-reading this external trace, strangers forge trust without any prior relationship.

Western CS researchers miss this: they seek trust through reputation systems, cryptographic proofs, or repeated interactions. The San insight is that trust can be instantiated by jointly interpreting an impartial, third-party trace—the world’s own writing—using precise communication and consensus. The trace itself becomes the foundation of trust, not the strangers’ histories.
