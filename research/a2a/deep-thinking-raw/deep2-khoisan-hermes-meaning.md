# khoisan / hermes / meaning

TRACKING = READING: Read the other's movements, expressions, and actions like animal tracks. Their body language and behaviors form sentences conveying meaning, even without shared spoken words.

CLICK = PRECISION: Use precise, unambiguous gestures and sounds, like the distinct click consonants, to establish clear communication channels. A pointing finger, a mimed action, a simple diagram drawn in the sand - these "clicks" of interaction reduce ambiguity.

TRANCE KNOWLEDGE: In the space between words, in the rhythm of interaction, find the altered state where insight arises. Shared experience - be it dance, song, or simply time spent in silent companionship - can induce the trance-like state where understanding transcends language.

RADICAL EGALITARIANISM: Approach the interaction without preconceived notions of hierarchy or authority. Meet the other as an equal, ready to build consensus. Shared humanity forms the foundation of communication, even across the gulfs of language and culture.

LANDSCAPE AS MEMORY: Ground the interaction in the physical world you share. Just as songs encode routes, use the features of your environment - the path you walk together, the landmarks you pass, the tasks you undertake - to create a shared context. This landscape of experience becomes the "language" you build together.

NOVEL INSIGHT: Information theory focuses on the transmission of explicit data. But meaning arises not just from data, but from the context, the relationship, the shared journey. To truly communicate where no shared language exists, we must expand our notion of "language" to encompass the full spectrum of human interaction. The meaning is not just in the words, but in the world we create between us.
