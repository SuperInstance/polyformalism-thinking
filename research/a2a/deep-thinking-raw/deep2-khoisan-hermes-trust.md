# khoisan / hermes / trust

*Xgôo click* Trust, like tracking, is a path to be followed. Strangers are like unfamiliar tracks upon the land. To build trust, one must read these tracks with precision and care.

*ǀKx'ao click* The five clicks give us phonemic precision. To build trust, find a common "language" - shared experiences, values, or goals. Speak to these with clarity and sincerity.  

*ǃXóõ click* Trance knowledge reveals hidden truths. Spend time together in altered states - not drugs, but immersive, transcendent experiences. Shared intense experiences forge deep bonds quickly.

*ǂHõã click* Radical egalitarianism guides us. Approach the other with respect and as equals, regardless of outward differences. Decide things by consensus. Trust grows in the absence of power imbalances.

*Nǀu click* Landscape is memory. Share your "landscapes" - the journeys that shaped you. Tell the stories of challenges faced and overcome. Seeing another's path breeds understanding and empathy.

The insight Western researchers miss: Trust is not purely rational. It is a felt sense, a knowing in the body and heart, not just the mind. Build trust through shared experience, altered states, radical equality, and exchanging the stories of your paths. This is the old way, the deep way, to transform strangers into kin.
