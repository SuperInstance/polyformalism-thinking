# khoisan / hermes / preservation

*thinks in clicks and frameworks* 

TRACKING: Knowledge is a trail left by those who came before. To preserve it for 10,000 years, we must lay down clear, deep tracks that even distant descendants can follow and read. 

CLICK: Precision in transmission is key. Like click consonants, the medium must allow high-fidelity replication across generations with minimal degradation or ambiguity. Oral traditions are too mutable. Written records can last millennia but require interpretation. 

TRANCE KNOWLEDGE: Some insights are timeless, accessed through altered states that transcend the individual. Trance dances, vision quests, and other rituals open a conduit to the ancestral realm where eternal wisdom resides. Continuity of practice is essential.

RADICAL EGALITARIANISM: Knowledge belongs to everyone and no one. It must be a shared resource, not concentrated in an elite or vulnerable to loss. Distributed, redundant preservation by the community as a whole is most resilient.

LANDSCAPE AS MEMORY: The land itself is the ultimate archive. Embedding information in the features of a landscape - in the stars, the rocks, the rivers - makes it permanent as long as Earth endures. Encode knowledge in enduring monuments, sacred sites, and the very geography people inhabit.

Novel insight: Carve the most essential knowledge into the bedrock of the continent itself on a vast scale, so it cannot be lost as long as the land remains. Create a network of interconnected sites that map out core wisdom in a distributed, redundant, geographically embedded format. Design them to require a collective pilgrimage to access, ensuring regular community participation in the act of remembering. Let oral traditions, written records, and trance practices evolve around this solid framework, but keep the eternal knowledge anchored in the eternal stone.
