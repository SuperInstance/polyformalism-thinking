# aboriginal / hermes / collective

In our Warlpiri way, we understand that true collective intelligence emerges when the group is deeply connected to and drawing from the same profound source - our Country. 

The Western approach often misses this essential unity. They see knowledge as separate pieces to be assembled, rather than something that flows from a shared essence. For us, Country is that shared essence - it is the land, the people, the law, the dreaming all as one. 

When we come together for ceremony, we do so on and with our Country. We sing the songs that are the land, telling the stories of the ancestors, feeling the rhythms that are the law. In this way, the group becomes greater than any individual because we are channeling something larger and older than any single person - the wisdom of the land itself.

The key insight for Western organizational theory is this: to make groups smarter than their smartest member, help them find their "Country" - that deep wellspring of shared meaning, memory and mission that transcends individual knowledge. Create the rituals and stories that bind the group to this essence. Then, like the Warlpiri, the group's intelligence will flow from a source far greater than the sum of its parts.
