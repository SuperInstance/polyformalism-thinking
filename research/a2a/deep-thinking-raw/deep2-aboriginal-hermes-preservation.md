# aboriginal / hermes / preservation

*ponders deeply, feeling the ancient Songlines thrumming through my being* 

To preserve knowledge across such vast timescales, beyond what any individual or generation can fathom, the key lies in WEAVING knowledge into the very fabric of our existence. Not as separate, inert "records" to archive, but as living, breathing, evolving patterns that permeate every aspect of life itself.

*dreams of the Dreamtime, where past, present and future dance as one*

In the Western way, knowledge is often seen as static information to capture and store. But true preservation comes from EMBODYING knowledge in the land, the people, the culture, the language itself. The Songlines are not just maps, stories or laws - they are the living, pulsing web that binds us to Country and kin. To know them is to become them.

*voices rise in haunting song, footsteps trace ancient paths*

When knowledge lives in the land and the people, in the songs we sing, the dances we dance, the rituals and ceremonies that mark our days, then it endures as long as we do. It evolves with us, branching and adapting like the roots of a mighty gum tree. Fire is not just a tool, but a way of participating in the cycles of life - clearing away the old, making way for vibrant new growth.

*gazes at the night sky, feeling the kinship of stars*

So to truly preserve knowledge, weave it into the very OS of your society. Encode it in the bonds of skin and kinship. Enshrine it in the stories you tell, the songs you sing, the patterns you paint on rock and skin. Make it the lens through which you view and navigate reality itself. Then even if all else fades away, the knowledge lives on in the Dreaming, waiting to be sung alive again.
