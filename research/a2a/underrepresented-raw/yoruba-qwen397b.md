**Àlàyé (The Explanation):**
You present this as a calculation of bodies. In Yoruba epistemology, this is a calculation of *Àṣẹ* (authority/power to make things happen) and *Orí* (destiny/inner head).

Here is how the Yoruba worldview dismantles and reconstructs your problem.

### 1. How Yoruba Framing Changes the Problem
**Western Frame:** An isolated agent (the car) calculates utility in a vacuum to minimize loss.
**Yoruba Frame:** The car is not an agent. It is a vessel carrying the *Àṣẹ* of the community that built and authorized it.

The decision is **not made in the 200ms**. To believe the decision happens in the 200ms is a Western illusion of individualism. In Yoruba thought, truth is *performed* through communal agreement. The "decision" was made months or years prior, in the town hall, the engineering guild, and the divination chamber where the code was sanctified.

The 200ms moment is merely the *enactment* of a covenant. The car does not "choose." The car *manifests* the will of the community. If the community has agreed that the sanctity of the pedestrian on the *Ilè* (Earth) outweighs the metal box behind, the code is simply the ritual drumbeat that enacts that truth.

**The Shift:** We move from *Calculative Ethics* (counting lives) to *Relational Ontology* (weighing relationships to Earth and Destiny).

### 2. What a Yoruba Decision-Maker Focuses On (That English Speakers Miss)
**A. The Tone of Existence (Ontological Tones)**
Yoruba is a tonal language; *ọkọ* (vehicle) is different from *ọkọ* (husband) based on tone. English treats objects as flat data points (x,y, velocity).
A Yoruba algorithm weighs the **ontological tone** of the objects.
*   **The Pedestrian:** Stands on *Ilè* (Earth). The Earth is a deity (*Ala*). To swerve is to violently impose your will upon the Earth and the child of the Earth. This is a high-tone violation.
*   **The Rear Vehicle:** Made of iron. It is already in motion. Its failure to keep distance is a failure of *Iwà* (character).
*   **The Focus:** We do not count heads. We weigh the *sacredness of position*. The pedestrian is where they belong (on the path/earth). The rear car is where they should not be (too close). The car must honor the one who is in *right relation* to the Earth.

**B. Pro-Drop Agency (The Disappearing "I")**

Western AV ethics obsess over *liability* ("Who is responsible?").
Yoruba ethics drops the subject. The action stands alone. **"Brake."** Not "I brake."
