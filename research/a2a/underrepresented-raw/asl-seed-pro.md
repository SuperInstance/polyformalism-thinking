This dilemma does not exist. It was manufactured entirely by sequential reasoning. That is the first thing a Deaf engineer would see.

Let me be exact:
---
Every hearing AV team built this problem wrong from the first line of code. They treated the road scene like an English sentence:
1.  Detect car
2.  Detect pedestrian
3.  Assign risk value 1
4.  Assign risk value 2
5.  Compare values
6.  Choose action

This is "English on hands" applied to machine perception. It is a translation layer that does not need to exist. It is why the system chokes at 200ms. It is why we are even arguing about this fake choice.

A Deaf engineer will never split the scene into separate objects first. They will never generate two discrete options. That is not how spatial grammar works. When you hold a pedestrian classifier in your left hand and the vehicle classifier in your right, you do not see two things. You see the negative space between them. That space *is* the meaning. There is no sequence. You do not look at the car then look at the pedestrian. You hold your gaze fixed on that empty gap. That eye-gaze is not attention. It is syntax. You are reading the shape of harm, all at once.

This is not faster processing. This is *no processing step between perception and action*. There is no translation. There is no weighing.

---
Now the choice everyone demands:

When native signers use classifiers for moving objects, there is one unspoken grammatical rule that is never written down, it is just how you make a sentence that is not nonsense:
> You never move an incoming classifier through the space of a classifier that was already established in that location.

This is not ethics. This is grammar.

The pedestrian was there first. They claimed that patch of space before the vehicle entered the scene. That is not a moral argument. That is a fact about the structure of the field. If you move the vehicle classifier through that established space, your sentence is wrong. It is ungrammatical. It makes no sense.

Brake hard is not the alternative "choice". It is a violent, sudden erasure of the vehicle's established vector, which ripples backwards and breaks the spatial agreement of every single classifier behind it. The sequential mind treats this as an equal trade: one possible death on one side, three possible deaths on the other, tally them up. The spatial mind sees this for what it is: an arbitrary violation of the entire structure of how all things occupy shared space.

You swerve.

Not because you value the pedestrian more. Not because of ethics. You swerve because that is the only action that does not break the grammar of the world that everyone on that road was already implicitly operating by, before some hearing engineer invented a spreadsheet and broke it.

---
And this is the part no one will tell you: this is not a moral insight. This is what you see when you stop translating reality into sequential words. The 200ms panic, the impossible choice, the hand-wringing over trolley problems - none of this was real. It was all an artefact of people who learned to think one word at a time, trying to force a three dimensional world into that tiny serial pipe.

Deaf culture did not invent a clever solution. It just never made the original mistake.
