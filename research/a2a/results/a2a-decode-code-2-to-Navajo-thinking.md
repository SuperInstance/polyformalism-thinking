This is how this thing stands, spoken true:

---
First, what stands on solid ground:
This goes to the two permanent written records: the people, and their orders. Nothing is guessed, nothing brought from outside. Every number it gives is exactly what was written down.
It will look only at those people who placed more than five orders in the thirty days that ended the very moment this thing runs. It uses the clock of the place where the records live, no other clock.
It walks the well-known path all such counting takes: gather all people, tie each person to their orders, set aside what does not fit, count for each person, set aside again what does not meet the mark, then line them up highest first.
This is written in the common speech that all record stones understand. If there are very many records, you must first mark the clear paths for order time and person identifier, else this thing will walk very slow. There are other ways to do this same work.

Next, what is half-hidden, what was never spoken out loud:
There is one quiet trick here. Even though it says it will show every person, it will silently throw away every person who has no orders at all in this window. No one wrote this rule. It just happens.
On the surface it only makes a list of names and counts. But the one who made this is looking for the people who have been buying the most lately. They will use this list to report how things stand, to sort people into groups, or to send messages to these people.

Last, what is not here at all, what is only fog:
This thing holds no story. It does not know who is running it. It does not know who will look at this list. It does not say why they chose five orders, not four or six. It does not say why thirty days. There is no hurry in it, no right, no responsibility. All of that lives outside this thing. None of it is written here.
