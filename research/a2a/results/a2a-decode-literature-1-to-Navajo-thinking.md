This is what is held here, nothing more:
There is an old man. There is the sea. They are old companions. This is all that lives inside this telling. No other people. No other land. No days outside these forty. Nothing but these two, and the fishing lines he sets.

Forty days have passed. Each day he lowers the lines. Every single time, they come up empty.

It is known the sea is kind. The sea gives good things. This nature of the sea did not change across all these days. Still it held back the fish. No one says why.

There is no fight here. No one broke word. No anger. The sea is the one that holds all power: it may give, it may hold back. The man waits. He does not quit. He keeps setting the lines.

There is quiet hurt sitting between them, unspoken. The hurt that comes when the one you have walked beside for so long does not feed you, even when they are still good. That is the thing under all this, that no one says out loud.

This is only a story. No one tells you who is speaking. You cannot go ask anyone if this happened. It is only the words that are given.

Only the lines are named. Nothing is said of how they are made. Nothing of bait. Nothing of what fish he seeks. Only that they exist, and they come up empty.
