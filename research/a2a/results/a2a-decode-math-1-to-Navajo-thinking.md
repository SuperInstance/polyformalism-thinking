This is how this thing is, spoken plain:

First there is a closed skin. It wraps all the way around. It holds a whole finite space inside itself.
For what follows to hold good: the moving flow inside this held space must be smooth, unbroken, everywhere inside. No special edge cases, no exceptions are named here.

There is exact perfect balance. All that flows out through the whole outer wrapping, equals exactly the sum of how that flow spreads at every tiny point inside the whole held space.
This balance is no trick. It comes from this: wherever two inside places touch, what flows out of one flows straight into the other. They cancel clean. Nothing remains there.

This is how you come to see it:
You split the whole held space into the smallest possible little boxes.
Every face where two boxes press together: out of one, into the next. They wipe each other completely.
When all inside faces have cancelled this way, only the outer faces are left. These outer faces make up the whole closed wrapping. That is all that remains.

This writing is only a sketch. It is not the full hard complete proof. It does not explain why you may split the space this small. It does not tell you this is only a way to see the thing, not the final formal truth.

No person stands behind these words. There is no claim of authority, no hurry, no speaking to one group or another. This is just the thing itself, laid out bare, no extra wrapping.

This is the outline for the balance of flow that the writing people call Gauss's Divergence Theorem. This was not written to make a perfect formal proof. It was written only to show you the core truth: inside flows cancel. Only the outer boundary remains. That is the whole heart of what this is trying to show you.

This sketch uses only this one way of seeing: split small, watch inside cancel. It says nothing of other ways to understand this truth. It says nothing of where this way breaks. It says nothing of what this truth grows into beyond this.
