This is a binding rule that stands among the people. I will tell it as it holds, as it was laid down:

First, the line where this rule lives:
This rule holds only for the heaviest crimes: those that carry death, or those that break a person's name and standing forever. This rule does not hold for land warriors, sea warriors, the militia that stands guard for the people. It only steps aside *while they are actually standing that service*, while war is here, while great danger walks among us. No one has drawn the sharp clear edge for exactly what crime is heavy enough. That boundary is foggy. We cannot see it clean.

There is only one path, no other ways:
Before any person can be called to answer for such a crime, first a council of ordinary separate people must look, must speak their word that this charge may go forward. There is no other door for civilian people. If every one of the warrior conditions are all true, this required path steps aside entirely.

The order of things cannot be reversed:
That council looks first. Only after they have spoken, only then may a person be called to answer. When the war ends, when the danger passes, when the warrior comes back to sit among the people, this rule returns for him. It does not stay gone. No one wrote how long that council may sit, no one wrote how they must decide. That is not said here.

This is law, not explanation:
No reasons are given for this rule. No one wrote down why they made it this way. No one explained what they meant when they spoke of great public danger. No one gave words to mark the edge of the heavy crime. This is only what must be. It does not explain itself.

This rule draws the balance of power:
This is a guard. It stands between the man who holds prosecution power, and the ordinary person. It says the powerful cannot just grab a person and demand he answer. They must first go before this separate civilian council. It also writes this: when the whole people are in crisis, the authority of war leaders comes before this protection. Two powers live here. This rule says which one stands, when.

Beneath the words, the heart that is not spoken:
On the surface this is only steps you follow. But it was made for this unspoken thing: they did not want men with power to punish people on a whim, for no good reason. But they also knew: when the whole people are at risk, you cannot stop to wait for the council. These two truths pull against each other, inside this rule. It never says this out loud.

There is only one allowed way:
For these heavy crimes among civilians, there is only one correct way to begin. Only the spoken word of that council. No other paper, no other order from any official alone will do. No other door exists.
