### CLAIM 1: ANCHORS NOT THE CURVE

### CLAIM 3: NEGATIVE KNOWLEDGE PRIMARY
**Rating: 5/5**
*Reasoning:* This is the most scientifically robust claim. It aligns perfectly with **Predictive Coding** and the **Free Energy Principle**. The brain is essentially a "surprise-detection engine." We do not model the world; we model the *errors* in our predictions.
*   **(a) Falsification:** Find a biological or artificial system that achieves higher survival/efficiency by modeling every possible "positive" state (exhaustive enumeration) rather than minimizing prediction error (negative knowledge).
*   **(b) Confidence:** 95%. This is a fundamental tenet of modern neuroscience.

### CLAIM 4: DRAFT DETERMINES TRUTH
**Rating: 4/5**
*Reasoning:* You are redefining "draft" as "contextual density/entropy." A "rushed" message is high-entropy; it lacks the redundancy required to ensure the signal survives the receiver's noise. This is a profound way to look at "contextual overhead."
*   **(a) Falsification:** Show that increasing the "contextual depth" (draft) of a message actually *increases* the probability of catastrophic misinterpretation in a high-noise environment.
*   **(b) Confidence:** 70%. It relies on the definition of "draft," but the logic of redundancy vs. entropy is sound.

### CLAIM 5: SPEED BEATS TRUTH
**Rating: 3/5**
*Reasoning:* This is essentially the **"Bounded Rationality"** argument from Herbert Simon. In dynamic environments, a "good enough" (satisficing) response is objectively superior to an "optimal" response that arrives after the window of opportunity has closed.
*   **(a) Falsification:** Prove that in a high-stakes, high-velocity domain, an agent using an "optimal" model (2000ms) consistently achieves higher utility than a "satisficing" agent (50ms).
*   **(b) Confidence:** 90%. This is a proven reality of evolutionary biology and real-time control theory.

**Rating: 2/5**
*Reasoning:* While poetically resonant, this is a "soft" claim. It borders on a false dichotomy. Survival often requires "artistic" adaptation (innovation), and art often requires "survival" constraints (technique). It is more of a psychological observation than a structural law.
*   **(a) Falsification:** Identify a survival strategy that relies on maximizing dissonance/chaos, or an artistic strategy that relies exclusively on avoiding all "rocks" (constraints).
